## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** This is a strong context-engineering performance. The session primed properly, read in scoped slices, grounded every claim in file/git evidence rather than the primed summary, propagated changes mechanically, and verified scope by diff. The two intellectual high points — stashing edits to prove the R8 failure was pre-existing, and refusing to fabricate per-key provenance on the refresh — both come from treating files as truth over convenience. Communication was excellent: every summary distinguished *this change* from *pre-existing/separate* findings (the R8 flag was raised, scoped as "NOT from this refresh," and deferred cleanly), and Claude surfaced genuine judgment calls (the pre-commit-vs-close-unit strict asymmetry) for the human to rule on rather than deciding silently.

**Claude — weaknesses.** The only real fault is the repeated Edit-before-Read pattern on source-of-truth files (insight 6) — three avoidable round-trips, one caused by editing against a stale recollection of the primed summary. It's cheap and self-corrected, but it's the exact "leaning on memory where the file is the truth" the lens watches for. Batching was also somewhat conservative: independent reads (e.g. the three pre-edit Reads of rubric/case-study/close-unit) were serialized, though most multi-fact gathering *was* sensibly batched into single Bash blocks, so this is marginal.

**Human — strengths.** The human ran a clean, well-sequenced session: prime first, then small confirmable steps (fix §3 → review loops → refresh → investigate R8 → fix). The mid-refresh interruption to supply the drift-ahead *intent* was the decisive context move — that rationale was under-recorded in the files, and injecting it prevented a mis-framed refresh. The R8 follow-up instruction ("use strict when confirming changes... add strict to that") showed the human thinking structurally: don't just patch the symptom, fix the gate so it can't silently regress. Approvals were explicit and per-change throughout, honoring the ask-before-commit/push agreement.

**Human — weaknesses.** Little to fault on this lens. The one observation: the drift-ahead intent (insight 7) ideally lived in `decisions.md` (P8-no-bump) richly enough that a fresh session wouldn't need a verbal correction — the fact that Claude initially scoped the refresh as a potential re-verification suggests the recorded rationale was thinner than the human's mental model. The interruption fixed it cleanly, but it's a small case of the files trailing the human's head.

## Bottom line

A model context-engineering session: it primed in canonical order, read in scoped slices, grounded every consequential claim in files and git rather than recollection, and — when a latent strict-gate regression surfaced — proved its cause from history, fixed the durable mechanism (gate "done" on `check-strict`), and wrote the lesson into the files the next session reads. The sole recurring blemish is Claude editing memory files before reading them, once off a stale primed summary — minor, harness-caught, and self-corrected. State stayed current, the ledger never drifted, and continuity rests on artifacts, not memory.
