## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** Across a very long session (626k/1m tokens at the midpoint) it never lost the thread, never re-read processed units, and never authored continuity from memory where a file or artifact was the truth. Every consequential design decision (session identity, back-fill mapping, the secret gate) was grounded in a fresh `jq`/`grep`/`env` probe rather than recollection. It batched independent shell work into single calls (status + diff + check together). It practiced rare context hygiene — count-only scanning, windowed match display — to avoid polluting the transcript it was archiving. And it treated the SKILL.md as the durable carrier of convention, fixing the file so the next session reads the right instruction.

**Claude — weaknesses.** The `sort`-then-re-run round-trip wasted one cycle. The early reliance on the AskUserQuestion picker worked against the human's capture goal until corrected. Neither is structural; both were self-caught.

**Human — strengths.** The human is the architect of this session's context discipline: he opened with a `/prime-context` skill, drove unit-at-a-time slicing with terse boundary prompts, and — most importantly — surfaced the lossy-capture problem himself ("I'm using this log as a training source and would like all my commands captured"), which is exactly the kind of "the conversation is not the record; the files are" realization the lens prizes. His design instincts were sharp and incremental: skill-now-not-hook, collapse thinking, "wait to back-fill until we've confirmed rendering," and pause-the-security-gate-before-pushing. He insisted names "best fit the content of the session, not just the first query" — pushing for durable, self-describing artifacts.

**Human — weaknesses.** Very little to fault on this lens. The deeper issue he uncovered — that his entire prior log corpus was captured via lossy `/export` — is a context-engineering miss, but it predates this session and he caught and remediated it here. If anything, he could have asked for the raw `.jsonl` capture earlier in the project; instead he learned it mid-stream and fixed it properly.

## Bottom line

This is the strongest possible session for the context-engineering lens: it both *demonstrates* good memory/window discipline (clean file-based priming, scoped reading, unit-sliced work, batched commits) and *manufactures* better continuity infrastructure than the project had — replacing a lossy `/export` ritual with a session-id-robust, secret-gated, raw-plus-rendered capture skill, and proving the renderer on the live session before trusting it. Claude consistently verified against artifacts instead of guessing, refused to fabricate a mapping it couldn't justify, and even guarded its own transcript from re-leaking secrets. The human set the frame and caught the foundational flaw. The only blemishes — one wasted `sort` round-trip and an early picker choice at odds with the capture goal — were both self-corrected. Through-line: when the source of truth is the files, build the files well — and this session did, recursively.
