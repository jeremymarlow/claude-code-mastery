## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**The human** ran this session the way a spec lead should: gates were active checkpoints where real decisions got made, not formalities. They expanded a requirement at the requirements gate, added and then deferred a second requirement with explicit sequencing ("I don't plan to do anything more with R19 until R18 is finished"), demanded a correctness sweep that found a genuine stale count, forced a grounded cost analysis before committing, and reopened the load-bearing panel decision deliberately. Their commit/push instructions were precise and per-change, which let Claude honor the no-standing-approval rule cleanly. The one thing to watch is that several of these interventions (panel composition, cost, the completeness-check reconsideration) arrived as successive after-the-fact reopenings; a habit of front-loading "here are the dimensions I care about — propose against all of them at once" could reduce review rounds. But that is a polish note on an already-strong process posture.

**Claude** held the discipline without being policed: it branched instead of committing to `main`, held spec edits for review, ran `make check` after every edit and reported PEND honestly as expected-not-a-regression, secret-scanned changed files, and — the standout — proactively caught and closed the §3/ledger drift before it could mislead the next session. Its slicing was clean (separate commits per concern, two-commit suggestions for clean history), its state-capture faithful (decisions rationale + ledger L11/L12 + §3 + tasks index, all pointing at the canonical ledger rather than spawning parallel lists), and its session-boundary reasoning was principled. It also modeled honest reversal twice and self-corrected its own arithmetic. The only soft spots are process-cosmetic: the panel churned across three rounds because the first proposal wasn't stress-tested for axis balance or cost up front, and AC6 was rewritten post-commit (safe on this branch, but a pattern to keep off `main`).

## Bottom line

This is a model spec-driven planning session: three real, separable gates held; scope cut into reviewable slices; commits disciplined and approval per-change; and project state kept honest — including a proactive catch of stale §3/ledger before it could poison the next session. Both parties earned did-well across process and communication. The only blemishes are cosmetic churn (panel re-litigated 8→12→11, an AC rewritten after its commit) on an unmerged branch where it costs nothing. Discipline this clean is exactly what the retrospective being planned here is meant to surface as the standard.
