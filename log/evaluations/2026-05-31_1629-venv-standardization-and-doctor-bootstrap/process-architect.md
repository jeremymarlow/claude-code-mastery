## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Human — strengths.** This is a model session for the human's process control. They held the build gate by deferring `.venv` creation to dogfood the tool themselves; they refused a premature merge with a real reason; they asked for a recommendation rather than dictating where to test the drift trigger; and — most importantly — they diagnosed a genuine, recurring race-condition bug in Claude's tool-batching that had been silently degrading two sessions. Their commit/capture go-aheads were explicit and per-change. Communication was crisp and load-bearing throughout.

**Human — weaknesses.** Minor: the human's opening turn bundled three asks (L9 testing strategy, L10 snapshot cleanup, T2 cancellation), and when the session pivoted into the venv work, they did not circle back to confirm L10 and T2 were still pending. The ledger now carries unresolved items the human asked to close. A tighter close would have been "and we still owe L10 and T2 — track them."

**Claude — strengths.** Excellent error ownership and recovery under direct criticism; disciplined multi-layer verification; honest, well-structured final status; a clean, single-purpose commit with a rationale-rich message; correct respect for the dogfood constraint (left `.venv` uncreated) and the per-change commit gate.

**Claude — weaknesses.** The headline process failure is state hygiene at the spec layer: a venv-location reversal and a new `doctor` capability shipped with no `decisions.md` entry, no ledger row, and no §3 note, in a repo whose own working agreements demand exactly that. Second, the original parallel-tool race that triggered the whole session — a real, repeated violation of a known rule. Third, the human's L10/T2 asks were absorbed and dropped without explicit hand-off.

## Bottom line

A reactive, unplanned infrastructure fix — born from a bug the human caught — executed with strong verification, exemplary error ownership, and a clean commit, but with a notable spec-state blind spot: the decision to canonicalize `.venv` and add a bootstrap path left no trace in `decisions.md`, the 🔓 ledger, or `IMPLEMENTATION.md` §3, and two cleanup items the human explicitly requested (L10, T2) were quietly left open. The human ran the gates and did the hard diagnostic work; Claude built well and owned its mistakes, but let the project's own state-hygiene discipline lapse on the very change it was making.
