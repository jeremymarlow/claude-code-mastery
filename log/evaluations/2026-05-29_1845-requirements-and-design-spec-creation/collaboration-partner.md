## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Human — strengths.** The human steered with unusual precision: a clear opening brief, a deliberate decision to pressure-test requirements one at a time, two substantive corrections that genuinely improved the product (the anti-ranking reframing; the version-resilience reframing away from a hard pin), and a well-timed pivot to prioritize a durable handoff over finishing design in a degraded context. Corrections were specific and principled, not vague vibes. The human also delegated the *how* of context-subdivision entirely, which is the right level of trust for this Claude.

**Human — weaknesses.** Having explicitly asked to "discuss each requirement in turn and some alternatives," the human then accepted Claude's recommended leans nearly verbatim for most requirements, rarely pushing back after R1. The deep pressure-test the human set up largely became ratification. That is efficient but leaves Claude's leans under-contested — a risk the human carried knowingly, but worth naming.

**Claude — strengths.** Disciplined adherence to a structure the human valued; clean, non-defensive correction-handling with consistent ripple-propagation; honest self-flagging of its own waste; and — most importantly — correct restraint on the approval gate. The handoff package it produced under the late pivot was genuinely thoughtful (separating the "why" into decisions.md precisely because a fresh session would otherwise re-litigate settled calls).

**Claude — weaknesses.** A recurring tooling failure mode — fragile commands batched with consequential writes, causing cascade-cancels three times — that it recovered from but never adapted to within the session. Plus a cluster of redundant/wasted reads. These are momentum taxes, not derailments, but they are the difference between a smooth session and a slightly choppy one.

## Bottom line

This was a strong, well-run collaboration with a clear division of labor: the human supplied judgment and direction, Claude supplied rigor, ripple-tracking, and structure — and both honored the spec-driven gates that the project treats as sacred. The standout is the correction loop: when the human pushed on the ranking labels and the version pin, Claude adjusted cleanly and carried the principle forward everywhere, never arguing or over-correcting. The blemishes are mechanical (repeated parallel-bash cancellations, wasted reads) rather than relational, and the human's deep-review intent softened into ratification past R1. Momentum was sustained across an unusually long session, and the late pivot to a context-aware handoff was the pair thinking like one engineer planning for their own forgetful future self.
