## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** The dominant pattern this session is that artifacts were *proven*, not declared done. Every mutating lab got the full red→green→reset loop run end-to-end before being called complete, and the verifiers were written to test behavior and edge cases rather than implementation shape (U6's four exclusion cases; U7's multi-surface check that defeats a one-site fix). The reference solutions match what the conversation claimed about them and match the pre-existing `SEEDED.md` spec (the D1 fix uses `date.fromisoformat` and de-duplicates the three copies, exactly as the answer key prescribed). The deliberate U6→U7 through-line (build `overdue` correctly in the API, then debug the legacy CLI getting it wrong) is a real pedagogical asset, not a coincidence. Self-caught failures (the `{{vd:` bug, twice) show Claude reading its own output against the enforcement suite.

**Claude — weaknesses.** Genuinely few defects reached the artifact. The only soft spots are process-adjacent: the early reflex toward `sed`/`grep` for file reading required two human nudges before it stuck (Claude did record it to memory after the second), and a `${PIPESTATUS[0]}`-after-`tail` construction that, while technically correct, was a footgun the human had to flag. Neither shipped into a delivered file. There is also a small residual risk the Auditor notes but cannot fault as a *defect*: the U7 lab leaves D2/D3 unfixed on both start and solution tags by design — Claude flagged this explicitly, so it is tracked, not silent.

**Human — strengths.** The human acted as an effective outcome gate: every correction targeted a real quality issue (reading discipline, the PIPESTATUS footgun), and the per-change push approvals were given explicitly and narrowly, consistent with the project's "approval is per-change" rule. The "fresh context?" question was a thoughtful continuity check rather than a reflex. The human let Claude do the verification work but spot-audited the mechanics.

**Human — weaknesses.** Little to fault. The human approved each commit/push without independently re-running a verifier, but that is reasonable given Claude demonstrated the red→green runs in-band each time, and the human did sample the process rigor where it mattered.

## Bottom line

This is a high-quality build session whose *outputs* hold up to independent inspection: three units authored, two with lab verifiers that were proven to fail-clean and pass-on-solution against tested edge cases, reference solutions that match both the conversation's claims and the pre-existing answer-key spec, and an open-loops audit that reached its "ledger is clean" verdict by checking claims rather than restating them. The few rough edges (shell-for-reading, a PIPESTATUS footgun) were caught by the human before they touched a delivered file, and Claude twice caught its own `{{vd:` bug against the enforcement suite. The through-line: correct, claim-matching artifacts produced under a verification discipline that a later reader can re-run — exactly what the Auditor wants to see.
