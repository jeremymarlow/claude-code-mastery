## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strong.** This is a near-model session for my lens. The defining moment is the JSON-twin episode: a passing `make check` was explicitly *not* trusted as proof, an unexpected `git status` was chased to root cause through reading `render-vd`, `_common.py`, and the JSON itself, and the fix was verified by re-grepping for the expected count. That is reading the artifact, not just running the tool. The provenance discipline was clean — keys flipped only on real evidence the human supplied, the `review-cmds` soft spot held until explicitly confirmed, and the two environment-blocked keys kept honestly `unverified` with documented blockers. Uncertainty was surfaced, not smoothed.

**Claude — the one real gap.** For a session about verification, never running `make check-strict` is the discordant note. CLAUDE.md calls strict the Definition-of-Done gate and warns that plain `make check` can stay green while a real regression lands; Claude even wrote "then `make check-strict`" into its own checklist — then declared done, and committed, on `make check` alone. The risk was low for this particular edit, but "done" was proven with the weaker instrument while the authoritative one sat unused. That, plus a couple of moments where confidence slightly outran the just-run evidence ("nothing outstanding is release-blocking"), is why communication lands did-okay rather than did-well.

**Human — strong.** The author did the irreplaceable part: the live `/help` verification, reported with exact scope ("all but 5, 6") and honest naming of *why* the blocked keys couldn't be confirmed, with the real output-styles docs pasted verbatim instead of summarized. They never pushed Claude to flip the unverifiable keys. The only thing I'd note is that the human, like Claude, gated the final commit/push without asking for strict — but the author isn't the one who wrote "run check-strict" into the plan, so that lands more on Claude.

Low stakes here, but the principle is the one this session exists to practice. did-well overall, with strict-gate discipline the single thing to tighten.
