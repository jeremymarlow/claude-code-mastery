## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** This is a model session for the verify-don't-trust lens on the *tooling* arc. Claude treated the `.jsonl` schema, the session-id env var, the snapshot/attachment/thinking surfaces, and every secret-scan hit as things to *check against the source*, never to assert from memory — and it explicitly invoked the repo's own R12.AC3 "verify the event name/payload against the CLI before wiring" rule when declining to build a `SessionEnd` hook on faith. It read its artifacts (rendered units, scan output, the JWT config) rather than trusting exit codes, caught real defects (token garbles, the Write-blob bloat bug in its own renderer), and reversed a wrong "bug" call honestly. Uncertainty was named, not buried (scan false negatives, "can't log the act of logging," the early-cluster ambiguity).

**Claude — weakness.** The single real lapse is the `check-strict` omission (insight 6). It is not cosmetic: the project memory loaded into this very session flags that plain `make check` once let a prose sweep silently drop a requirement reference, and this *was* a prose sweep. Declaring "P7 COMPLETE" across four state files on the weaker gate is a "done before proving it" by the project's own definition. A secondary nit: a few rendered-transcript edits (the `mkdir -p` SKILL.md edit shown as a no-op old==new) aren't independently verified in the transcript, though the file-state confirmations cover them.

**Human — strengths.** The human drove the highest-value verification of the session: catching that `/export` was lossy and refusing the guessed explanation, then forcing the riskiest action (publishing raw transcripts) through a deliberate security pause with a human-in-the-loop line-check gate. The instruction to make the gate "a prompt from you for the user to check a specific line" institutionalizes verify-before-commit. The "pause on everything" call and "ensure the self describing names best fit the content of the session, not just the first query" both push for higher fidelity over speed.

**Human — weakness.** The human approved each commit batch and the close-out on Claude's "CHECK GREEN" reports without ever asking for `check-strict` — the gate their own CLAUDE.md designates for "done." A "did you run strict?" at the 7.9 close-out would have closed insight 6. This is mild: the human's verification energy was (reasonably) spent on the novel, irreversible security/publishing risk rather than the well-trodden mechanical gate.

## Bottom line

The through-line is verify-before-acting applied with real rigor where it mattered most — the human forced a halt before the one irreversible, high-exposure step (publishing raw transcripts), and Claude met it by grounding every design and security claim in inspected data rather than assertion, reading its artifacts, and reversing a wrong call honestly. The lone blemish is that "done" for the whole phase was declared on plain `make check` when the project's own Definition-of-Done is `make check-strict` — the exact weaker-gate trap CLAUDE.md warns about for prose sweeps. Strong evidence discipline overall, with one self-inflicted gap at the finish line.
