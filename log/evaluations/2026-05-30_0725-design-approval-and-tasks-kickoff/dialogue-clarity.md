## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**The human** ran a textbook instruction loop: every directive was short, scoped, and decisive, with no contradictions or scope drift. The standout contribution was using *questions* as instruments — the continuity-tracking audit and the end-of-session knowledge-handoff prompt both extracted higher-value work than a plain "do X" would have, and the venv interrupt was a clean, specific correction delivered without ceremony. The only soft spot is a mild reliance on implicit delegation for a few small calls (the gitignore fix, awareness home-units), which Claude had to infer; stating those grants, or expecting a pre-action flag, would tighten the loop marginally. Nothing here cost the session.

**Claude** communicated with genuine discipline. Replies led with the conclusion, used tables for scannable inventories, and closed with a single decisive choice. It surfaced tradeoffs and deferrals as flagged decisions (the U14 hook, the PENDING/strict gate, `--no-ff`) instead of either silently complying or silently gold-plating. It reported failure honestly: two bugs found during verification were named, the unverifiable version keys were quantified and marked, and — most impressively — when asked whether continuity tracking was adequate, it led with "Partially" and self-incriminated rather than reassuring. Sycophancy was essentially absent; the couple of upbeat asides were tethered to real outcomes and cost no clarity. The weakest edge is a faint tendency toward self-narrated wins ("verifying against the real CLI paid off immediately"), which a stricter reviewer would trim, but it never crossed into hollow flattery.

## Bottom line

This is a model collaboration on the clarity axis. The human gave crisp, decisive instructions and — more valuably — asked two probing audit questions at exactly the right moments; Claude answered with lede-first, tradeoff-surfacing, honestly-self-critical replies, capped by a continuity-fix it diagnosed against its own work and a clean session handoff. The through-line is that both parties treated the *dialogue* as a tool for de-risking a multi-session build: questions that invited judgment, answers that volunteered the uncomfortable truth. The only blemishes are minor — a little implicit delegation from the human and a few self-congratulatory asides from Claude — neither of which dented the exchange.
