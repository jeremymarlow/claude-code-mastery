## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** The session is a clean example of alignment done by anticipation. The single most valuable move was front-loading the hard constraint (headless can't verify in-REPL) so the human understood their role before committing time. The checklist turned a vague ledger item into an enumerated, testable instrument, which made the human's confirmation trivially mappable and convergence near-instant. Claude inferred the close-out scope correctly without an extra confirmation round, kept the unverifiable keys honestly open, and proactively surfaced the one item (`review-cmds`) the human hadn't addressed. Even the secondary catch — noticing the stale §3 branch line during prime-context and folding the fix in — shows it was tracking what "done and accurate" really required.

**Claude — weaknesses.** Very little on the alignment axis. The closest thing to a slip is process-adjacent rather than intent: `make render` was assumed to regenerate the JSON twin, and Claude only caught the stale `.json` via a self-check after the fact — but this is execution, not a misread of the human's intent, and it was caught and corrected within the session. The "save this as a working file?" offer was well-judged (asked, didn't assume), though it added a small decision the human never had to make since they verified in one pass.

**Human — strengths.** The human communicated with high signal and low overhead. "I can confirm all but 5, 6" plus the precise reason for each exclusion (no GitHub Action via Gitea, no enterprise account) gave Claude exactly what it needed to act correctly — including the crucial *why* behind the two it couldn't confirm, which is what let Claude record honest blockers rather than guess. The human also volunteered richer detail than asked (the full output-styles description, `/rewind`, the deprecation history), which let Claude sharpen `output-styles` well beyond its prior thin entry. The redirect at the top ("prep the L1 verification checklist") was a crisp, unambiguous pick from the two options Claude offered.

**Human — weaknesses.** Minor and self-corrected: the first verification message omitted `review-cmds`, which Claude had to prompt for — an earlier scan of the full checklist would have closed it in one turn. The instruction to "commit this and push" combined two actions Claude was careful to keep separate, but since it was explicit and per-change, no harm.
