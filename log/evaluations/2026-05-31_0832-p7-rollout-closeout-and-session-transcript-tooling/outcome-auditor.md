## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** This is a strong outcome session. The three infrastructure artifacts are correct, defensive, and durable; the bug Claude shipped in v1 it caught and fixed in the same session; the bug it *thought* it shipped it disproved with evidence. Its security reasoning was empirical throughout — verifying session-id resolution, anatomizing the `.jsonl`, checking how the sample app sources its secret — and it disclosed its own tool's false-negative unprompted. Claims matched files: commit diffstats, "green" assertions, and the closeout amendments were all backed by visible runs, and stale amendment notes were reconciled after the dir-split. The de-coding rollout across U5–U16 was mechanical but careful, with version-token garbles fixed at source and called out in commit messages.

**Claude — weaknesses.** The one real outcome-process gap is validating with plain `make check` rather than `make check-strict`, despite CLAUDE.md naming this exact scenario (a P7 prose sweep dropping a sole requirement reference) as the reason strict exists. The prose edits look sound and CI is a backstop, but the mandated DoD gate was skipped before declaring units done — the kind of thing an independent check is supposed to be the last defense against. Minor: the in-session render used file-mtime for date prefixes (later changed to first-event timestamp), so the dates in the session's own discussion don't all match the final committed filenames — harmless, but a small claim-drift.

**Human — strengths.** The human drove quality outcomes directly: the "pause to consider all security concerns" interjection forced the right review at the right moment (just before committing raw transcripts), and the two late edge-case prompts (concurrent/cross-machine safety; arc-based default naming) are exactly the gaps that would have made the shipped skill subtly wrong. The "will it be clear to you…" question is model outcome-auditing of Claude's artifact. Decisions were crisp and unambiguous ("A, content-fit names, batched gate, this branch"; "clear them; pause on everything").

**Human — weaknesses.** Light. The human accepted "make check green" as the done-signal without invoking the project's own strict gate — understandable since Claude offered it that way, but the human is the one who wrote the strict-gate agreement into CLAUDE.md. Also, the long run of bare "continue to U7/U8/…" prompts delegated heavily without spot-checking individual de-coded units, relying on the U1–U4 precedent and CI; it worked, but it was trust extended on process rather than verified output.
