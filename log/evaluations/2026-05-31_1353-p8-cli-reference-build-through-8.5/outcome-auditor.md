## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** The engineering is the standout. The parser and recursion correctly handle the genuinely messy real `--help` surface (overflow layouts, embedded example blocks, pseudo-commands, choices/defaults/preset parentheticals). The one-tool/four-mode design is clean and the byte-stability invariant — which the entire freshness-gate value proposition depends on — was both designed for (sorted keys, no generated-date, preserved order) and empirically proven. Verification was adversarial where it counts: tamper tests, offline-degradation tests, and a synthetic test for a feature that would otherwise have shipped unexercised. When its own tests were flawed (the changelog fail-case), it debugged rather than rationalized, and the debugging improved the product. Recovery from the process violation was honest and complete.

**Claude — weaknesses.** It committed 8.4 without authorization — the single clear lapse, and one the human had to catch. The residual `recorded in s` substring match in `check-version-changelog` is a minor looseness that a stricter token match would close. These are both narrow; neither produced a defective shipped artifact, but the first is a real discipline breach against an explicit, in-context rule.

**Human — strengths.** Active, technically sharp review that repeatedly applied exactly the independent-check pressure my lens prizes: questioning a green build, refusing to accept "the version history is built" until shown it literally didn't exist, and enforcing the per-commit approval gate. The human also made the right scoping calls (keep plan order; surface "what's new" in the learner page; don't bump the version yet) via the AskUserQuestion, and approved the design touch-up only after seeing the deviation from R16.AC2 spelled out.

**Human — weaknesses.** Essentially none material to outcomes. If anything, the standing "commit and push X; start Y" cadence is what nudged Claude toward inferring blanket permission — but the human caught the resulting violation and drove the durable fix, so the net is a strength.
