## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**The human** ran a near-textbook delegation: ceded all mechanical work, reserved energy for judgment calls, and used short, pointed questions to surface latent problems (the prop artifacts, the `close-unit` bug). The human also calibrated trust correctly over the session — by the end, "double check our skills... then push everything if no changes are needed" was a large, multi-step delegation that Claude had earned. The only critique is mild: the human approved the original `new-unit`/`unit-check` artifacts at the plan gate (via the AskUserQuestion preview) without interrogating consumer-value then, which is what set up the two reworks — though one could equally read the human as deliberately letting Claude proceed and then sharpening iteratively, which is a legitimate style.

**Claude** was an excellent partner: well-judged autonomy, immaculate gate-discipline on commit/push, honest about costs and uncertainty, and — most impressively — it generalized corrections into standards rather than treating each as a one-off patch. Its weakness is the flip side of its agreeableness: it accepted the initial artifact set without the skeptical "but who actually uses this?" pass it was fully capable of (it had quoted R14 "authentic dogfooding" verbatim early on), which cost two commits. A more proactive partner raises that test *before* the first commit. Secondary nit: occasional over-length in replies.

## Bottom line

A high-functioning pair operating at the top of its game on a subtle problem: what does "authentic dogfooding" mean when the tool's user is an AI, not a human? The human steered with a few surgical questions; Claude answered each with candor, fixed real defects (including a bug in its own dogfooded command), held every commit/push gate, and — the thing that most defines a good partner — turned corrections into a reusable standard instead of relitigating them. The single avoidable flaw was that Claude's "is this artifact a prop?" insight arrived two commits late, surfaced by the human rather than by Claude's own pre-commit skepticism. Net: clean correction loops, earned trust, sustained momentum, modest avoidable churn.
