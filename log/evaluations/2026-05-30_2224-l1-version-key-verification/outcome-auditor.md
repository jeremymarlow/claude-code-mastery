**Claude — what didn't.** Only nits. It gated on `make check` rather than the strict DoD gate its own checklist named (immaterial here, but the agreement is the agreement). And the commit was a co-authored push to `main` directly — appropriate given the explicit "commit this and push," but worth noting the pre-commit hook (not strict) was the only automated backstop before the push landed.

**Human — what worked.** Sequenced the work correctly (checklist before close-out), did the real verification Claude structurally could not, reported back with precise data, was candid about environmental limits, and gated commit/push behind an explicit instruction. **Human — what didn't.** Nothing material. The human could optionally have asked for `make check-strict` confirmation before authorizing the push, but the change class made that low-risk.
