## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**The human author.** Strong on alignment throughout. The author's signature move is feeding work in small, gated increments and letting Claude restate scope before each — which is exactly the structure that prevents silent-assumption drift. The mid-stream nudges were surgical (tooling/method, never deliverable intent), evidence that the upstream spec investment had already settled the "what." Push/commit gating was crisp and per-change ("push start/u06-lab1 and solution/u06-lab1", then separately "push the spec/tasks-phase branch as well", then "push it all"), honoring the project's per-change-approval rule and never leaving Claude guessing. The one place the author could have pulled more value: nothing forced the U7 D1-only scope decision to the surface before the build — but that's a shared gap, and the author's responsiveness when scope *was* raised (U8) shows they'd have engaged readily. The closing "Should we start with a fresh context…" was a thoughtful, well-judged process question.

**Claude.** Excellent convergence behavior. It consistently read the authoritative artifacts before proposing, restated each deliverable as a concrete, testable contract before building (the `overdue` predicate edge-cases; the U7 "fix at every site" criterion; the U8 lab-shape reasoning), and reserved questions for genuinely discretionary scope (the WebFetch offer, the U8 implicit lab-shape decision it resolved from spec). Its closeouts were models of alignment communication: what shipped, how it was verified, what design calls were made, and a *proposed* next step that left the boundary with the human. The red-then-green verification on each lab is the "what" being made objective. The lone soft spot is symmetry — the U8 judgment call was flagged pre-emptively and the U7 one only post-hoc; applying the same "surface scope decisions in the plan" reflex uniformly would close the gap.

## Bottom line

This is what high-quality intent alignment looks like when the upstream spec is doing its job: the "what" arrived pre-elicited and testable, Claude loaded it before acting, restated each deliverable as a concrete contract before building, and reserved its (few, well-judged) questions for genuinely discretionary scope. The human gated work into clean single-unit slices and corrected only method, never intent — the clearest signal that convergence was solid. Across an audit and three authored units there was zero intent-level rework. The only refinement worth naming is symmetry in surfacing scope decisions: the U8 lab-shape call was flagged before building, the U7 D1-only call only after — surfacing both in the plan would have made an already-strong session airtight.
