## Per party — recurring strengths & failure modes

**Human author:** Steady `did-well` on process across the whole corpus, `did-okay`/`did-well` on communication. Recurring strengths: tight per-change gate discipline; high-leverage skeptical interventions at exactly the right moment; converting one-off bugs into durable systemic fixes (venv→doctor bootstrap, R8→strict-gate, batch-warning→pre-emptive reminder); reserving the genuinely strategic forks for himself while delegating execution judgment. The one stable soft spot is communication terseness — minimal directives that lean entirely on Claude's context-reconstruction (and, twice, an information-free "please continue" after a hang). It always worked, but it offered little steering signal and no acknowledgement when Claude surfaced trade-offs, and it occasionally let scope drift uncommented (the four-unit `2026-05-30_1703` marathon).

**Claude:** A genuine split. Strengths are remarkably consistent across all 23: empirical verification, file-over-memory grounding, version-currency honesty, real dogfooding rigor, faithful state-sync, escalating true design forks, and non-defensive recovery. The failure modes are equally consistent and cluster in two bands: (1) **mechanical tool hygiene** — oversized/duplicate/dependent-in-one-batch calls that it was specifically pre-warned against and still repeated; (2) **front-of-loop judgment** — shipping props that failed the authenticity bar, sliding into infrastructure ahead of the spec, inferring commit permission from rhythm, a confident-then-reversed enforcement recommendation. The pattern: Claude's *output* almost always earns trust because verification catches errors before the human sees them, but the *path* to that output was, in roughly a third of sessions, noisier or less disciplined than it should have been — and the discipline lapses were ones the project's own memory and rules had already tried to prevent.

## Bottom line

The corpus-level through-line is a **well-instrumented, high-trust collaboration where the human's steady skeptical supervision repeatedly compensated for Claude's mixed process hygiene, and Claude's relentless verification-and-candor compensated for everything else.** The work product is consistently strong — nearly every session shipped correct, verified, spec-faithful artifacts — but that quality was load-bearing on two things outside autonomous Claude execution: the human's well-timed interventions (which produced most sessions' best moments) and Claude's own verification reflex (which caught its errors before they shipped). The single most telling longitudinal fact is that Claude's dominant failure mode — self-inflicted tool-batching chaos — recurred across multiple sessions *despite a durable memory file written expressly to stop it*, showing that learned-and-recorded lessons did not reliably translate into in-the-moment behavior. Trajectory: human steady-strong, Claude mixed (high, stable ceiling on verification and candor; recurring, pre-warned floor on mechanical hygiene and front-of-loop judgment).
