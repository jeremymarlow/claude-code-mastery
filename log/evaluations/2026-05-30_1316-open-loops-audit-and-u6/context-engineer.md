## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** This is close to a reference performance for the context-engineering lens. The orient-before-act reflex was unbroken across four units; reading was scoped to the task and paginated rather than dumped; state was externalized to every canonical surface after every unit so continuity never depended on the conversation; and the open-loops audit was conducted entirely against files and live CLI state, never memory. The end-to-end lab verification (red-on-clean, green-on-solution, reset-restores-clean) was disciplined, and Claude correctly distinguished the prose-self-check labs (U8, like U2/U4) from mutating ones — a decision driven by real environmental facts (`gh` not installed, R7.AC7 forbids external deps) rather than convenience. The fresh-context conversation showed it understands *why* window management matters, not just the mechanics.

**Claude — weaknesses.** The early shell-first-for-reading habit and a serialized-call pattern both contradicted feedback memories that were already loaded, requiring human nudges to surface. Minor, self-corrected, and turned into durable feedback files — but a more memory-aware start would have avoided them. A small Bash footgun also needed the human's catch.

**Human — strengths.** The human acted as an effective context coach: each intervention targeted a transferable principle (read-tool over shell, file-state awareness, PIPESTATUS correctness) and was phrased as a question that prompted Claude to reason rather than just comply. Push approvals were given per-change exactly as the working agreements require, and the "fresh context?" question was a thoughtful, well-timed prompt that elicited genuine analysis. Directives were terse and unambiguous.

**Human — weaknesses.** Essentially none within this lens. If anything, the human could have caught that the shell-reading habit was a known, memory-logged issue and pointed Claude at the existing memory rather than re-teaching it — but that is a quibble against an otherwise exemplary supervisory posture.

## Bottom line

A model session for context discipline: Claude primed against the live status file before every action, read in tight task-scoped slices, kept context nearly flat across four full units, and externalized state to every canonical surface so continuity rests on files rather than recollection — verified rather than remembered. The human reinforced exactly the right habits with sharp, principle-targeting nudges and correct per-change push gating. The only blemishes are a shell-first-for-reading reflex and serialized calls that contradicted already-loaded feedback memories and needed correcting — small lapses in an otherwise textbook run.
