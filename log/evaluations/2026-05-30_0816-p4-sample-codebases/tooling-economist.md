## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** The dominant story is economy. Tools were chosen by fit, not habit: targeted greps to scope reads, ranged file reads instead of whole-file pulls, Write/Edit/Bash for an authoring job that needed nothing heavier, and a `TodoWrite` plan sized to the actual five-part structure. The smoke-test-then-suite sequencing (insight 2) and reproduce-don't-assert verification of the seeded bugs (insight 3) are the kind of cheap-failure-first ordering this lens prizes. The pre-commit hook ran `make check` automatically on commit, closing the loop without an extra manual turn. Throughout, Claude respected the repo's verification gates and committed only on explicit instruction.

**Claude — weaknesses.** Three soft spots, all minor. The spec-state finale serialized into more single-action turns than necessary (insight 5). A handful of authoring slips — guessed line numbers, a stray non-English fragment, a docstring port mismatch — created small corrective rework that grep-first authoring would have pre-empted (insight 6). And two pieces of leverage went unused: a subagent for the standalone legacy CLI (insight 8), and arguably a cheaper model for the rote bulk (insight 7).

**Human — strengths.** Correct, disciplined gating: terse task kick-off, then a deliberate separate approval for the commit, exactly matching the project's per-change-approval rule. No premature push, no scope-creep prompts.

**Human — weaknesses.** Very low steering bandwidth. The author delegated essentially all tool/feature and judgment decisions to Claude and engaged only at start and commit boundaries. That worked here because Claude's defaults and framing were strong, but it means the human contributed little to the tooling economy of the session — including not weighing in on the LOC-vs-spec call that Claude explicitly opened for input.

## Bottom line

A disciplined, economical build session: Claude reached for the right-sized tool at nearly every step, sequenced verification to fail cheap, and tracked a multi-part deliverable cleanly to a green commit. The leverage left on the table is modest — a few over-serialized bookkeeping turns, a little avoidable authoring rework, and an unused subagent/model-tiering opportunity on a context-heavy, largely-mechanical job. The human's contribution was correct but thin: good gating discipline, minimal steering. Net, this is strong Claude Code fluency with a couple of efficiency notes rather than any real misuse.
