**Claude — to improve.** Batching was uneven: the long edit sequences serialized independent Edits into many round-trips where a single block would have done. And while reads were scoped, a few orientation Reads relied on the tool's truncation cap rather than a deliberate offset — fine here given the headroom, but a habit worth tightening on a tighter budget. Communication graded did-okay only because the closing summaries, though accurate, were long and somewhat repetitive across the gate closes (every gate ended with a multi-section recap); a leaner close would have spent fewer tokens for the same continuity value.

**Human — strong.** The author managed the process and the context budget actively: deliberate pauses on the load-bearing decision, a correctness-sweep demand, and — most valuably for this lens — forcing the token-load investigation *before* committing, which is precisely "fit the work to reality, don't discover it mid-run." Instructions were clear, decisive, and gated approvals per-change (honoring the working agreement), and the session-boundary question deferred to Claude's read of its own context in good faith.

**Human — to improve.** Pasting full Context Usage blocks three times added avoidable noise to the window. Minor, and arguably a deliberate health check, but the full table was more than the moment needed.
