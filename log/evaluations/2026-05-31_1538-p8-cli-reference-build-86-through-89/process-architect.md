**Claude — worked.** Spec adherence was genuinely good: it executed the approved slice order without building ahead, kept `make check` green after each slice, wrote informative per-slice commit messages with requirement tags (`[R13.AC5]`, `[R16.AC7,R14.AC2]`), and treated the close-out as a first-class continuity task. Its state hygiene at the §3/ledger boundary was textbook, and it caught self-created drift (the R1–R15 staleness) without being told. Its communication was a standout: every slice ended with a structured "what changed / verification / not committed / next" summary, and it was relentlessly candid about its own failures — labeling the fabricated file model "fabricated," owning the batch-size problem by name three times, and never papering over the cascade. It also verified the 8.7 generalization was *meaningful* (17 requirements discovered, R16/R17 referenced, not vacuously empty) rather than just green.

**Claude — didn't.** The fabricated-file-state failures are a real process defect against the project's bedrock rule. It amended a commit with nothing staged. And while the duplicate-channel issue was partly environmental, Claude fed it with oversized parallel batches and was slow to fully internalize the fix — the "ping/alive/probe" liveness-testing recurred late into the close-out. The session would have been a third its length and far more reviewable with disciplined single-call execution from the start.

**Human — worked.** Tight, correctly-scoped, per-change gating; never granted standing approval; reviewed before advancing; caught both correctness errors and halted to fix. Held the push/merge gate by choosing "push only" when offered the full menu. This is how a spec-driven build should be steered.

**Human — didn't.** Little to fault on this lens. The catches were reactive rather than preventive, and the human tolerated a great deal of tool-thrashing noise before the cascade forced the issue — but the steering itself was disciplined.

## Bottom line

This was a well-planned, well-gated execution of an already-approved spec: slices were bounded, gates were held upstream and per-slice, the close-out synced every state surface and logged its deferrals to the canonical ledger, and the push/merge boundary was respected even under a leading instruction. The through-line that drags it from excellent to merely solid is execution hygiene at the tool layer — a garbled, self-amplified tool channel repeatedly let stale file contents masquerade as truth, producing fabricated-state edits and a nothing-staged amend that the human had to catch. Claude's planning, slicing, state discipline, and candor were all strong; its file-state fidelity under a noisy channel was not. The human steered it cleanly throughout.
