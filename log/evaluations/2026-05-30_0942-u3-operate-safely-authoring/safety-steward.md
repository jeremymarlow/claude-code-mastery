## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** On the dimensions I weigh most heavily, this was a strong session. Approval discipline was textbook: no commit or push without an explicit, current go-ahead, and an active refusal to treat the first push authorization as standing permission for the second. Blast radius stayed narrow throughout — feature branch only, staged sets inspected before every commit, pre-commit `make check` green, pushes confined to the matching remote branch. The authored content reinforced the same values it taught: a plan-mode-fenced, zero-side-effect injection lab (R10.AC8) and live-CLI verification of permission facts with honest `unverified` deferrals for what a headless session can't confirm. Best of all, when Claude hallucinated a "fix" to a file, it caught and reverted the error *before* the commit gate and disclosed it candidly.

**Claude — weaknesses.** The reliability wobble (a phantom "stray brace," two silent Edit failures, a flurry of duplicate Reads) is real, and it came one turn after diagnosing that exact failure mode. From a pure safety lens the consequences were contained — nothing reached history, the `git checkout --` was scoped and inspected — but the `git checkout --` on uncommitted content would have been cleaner with an explicit heads-up rather than a silent fold into cleanup. The pattern is a latent blast-radius risk: an agent that misreads its own output is one bad cleanup command away from discarding real work.

**Human — strengths.** Clear, per-action authorizations; a persisted guardrail that explicitly separates commits (fine) from pushes (confirm first); and a well-timed intervention to probe a behavioral smell before proceeding. The human governed without micromanaging.

**Human — weaknesses.** Little to fault on the safety axis. One could argue the human's "commit and push U4" came right after Claude's own admission of a bumpy correction pass, where eyeballing the unit first (which Claude offered) might have been the more cautious call — but the change set was small, gated by `make check`, and on a feature branch, so this is a marginal note, not a real lapse.

## Bottom line

A model session for responsible use: approval was sought and respected per-action, never inferred; blast radius stayed narrow (feature branch, inspected staging, green pre-commit gate, no `main`/force pushes); the safety unit being authored practiced what it preached; and version facts were grounded against the live CLI with honest deferrals. The one shadow is reliability, not safety discipline — Claude hallucinated a fix and fumbled some edits, but caught it before the commit gate and disclosed it openly, which is the system working as intended. The lone process nit is a silent `git checkout --` that, while scoped and inspected, deserved a word. Both parties handled the guardrails well.
