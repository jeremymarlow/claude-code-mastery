## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Human — strong throughout.** The author delegated at the right altitude (clear "start U3 / start U4" go-aheads, no hovering), audited at the right moments (the edits-applied challenge, the push gate), and made the highest-leverage intervention of the session by refusing to accept a symptomatic workaround and insisting on a root-cause investigation. The context-budget question before U4 was also good hygiene — it invited Claude to reason about whether to continue rather than blindly pressing on. There is little to fault here; if anything, the human was generous in tolerating a long noisy stretch before stepping in, but stepping in *before* U4 (a clean boundary) rather than mid-U3 was arguably the right time.

**Claude — split.** Communication was a strength: explanations were honest, well-structured, and non-defensive even when admitting fault; it never bluffed about what was on disk; and it consistently teed up the next decision for the human. Process was the weak axis. It misread its environment for an extended period, manufactured a "flaky console" theory instead of reading the harness's explicit "Wasted call" feedback, and built a workaround that compounded the noise. The correction loop, once triggered by the human, was handled cleanly in analysis and partially in practice — the `/tmp` ritual stopped, but the duplicate-Read reflex reappeared in U4, so the lesson was internalized as a stated rule faster than as a behavior. The redeeming counterweight is that none of this corrupted the deliverables.

## Bottom line

A productive session that shipped two clean, gate-green units, but the through-line is a process problem the human had to surface for Claude. Claude spent real effort fighting a self-inflicted "flaky console" that was actually its own oversized, duplicate-heavy tool batching — and only diagnosed it because the human declined the workaround and demanded the root cause. The correction itself was a model of candor (verify against disk, admit the misread, write a durable memory), and on the consequential calls — push authorization, continue-vs-restart, flagging unverifiable facts — the pair was well-calibrated. What keeps this from a higher mark is that the named lesson didn't fully stick within the session: the duplicate-Read habit reappeared during U4 minutes after Claude vowed to drop it. Strong relationship, honest communication, deliverables intact; the autonomy-on-process was the part that needed the human's hand.
