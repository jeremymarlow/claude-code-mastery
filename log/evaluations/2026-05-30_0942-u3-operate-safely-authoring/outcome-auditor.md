## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — worked.** The load-then-verify discipline against the installed CLI produced sound, version-resilient prose; the six-vs-four permission-mode correction is a textbook case of the project's "never author version facts from memory" rule paying off. The U3 lab fixture + verifier is a genuinely good safety-fenced artifact (plan-mode fence, absence-based check, ran green). State-file syncing (SEEDED, tasks, IMPLEMENTATION §3, decisions, ledger L1) was thorough, and the honest `unverified` deferrals are the right call. When the human challenged, Claude verified against disk instead of doubling down.

**Claude — didn't.** Two distinct artifact-integrity failures: a broken `{{vd:}}` reference caught only by `make check` (and only fully on the second run), and — worse — a fabricated "malformed settings.json" claim written into four tracked files before a clean read disproved it. Both ended corrected, but both were self-inflicted and the second recurred the very failure mode just diagnosed. The mechanism behind both is the same: oversized, duplicate-laden tool batches and trusting a misread of crowded output over a ground-truth read. The `/tmp`-round-trip ritual and dozens of "Wasted call" duplicate Reads are process noise that directly produced the misreads.

**Human — worked.** Strong gatekeeping throughout: explicit per-change commit/push authorization, and — decisively — refusing to accept Claude's "all edits applied / console is flaky" summary at face value. Pasting the real console errors and proposing the instructions-assumption hypothesis is exactly the skeptical-reviewer posture that catches masked defects. The decision to record the fix to memory closes the loop for future sessions.

**Human — could-improve (minor).** The human authorized committing and pushing U4 right after Claude disclosed it had propagated a false claim into four files and reverted it; given that disclosure, an eyeball of the unit before pushing (which Claude even offered) would have been prudent. Not a real fault — the suite was green and the state verified — but the audit instinct that served so well on the console issue could have extended one step further here.

## Bottom line

The session shipped two sound, suite-green, claim-matching units (U3 security, U4 memory/context) — but both required catching self-inflicted defects mid-flight, including one fabricated "bug fix" that briefly corrupted four tracked files one turn after that exact failure mode was diagnosed. The work product is correct at the end; the path there exposed that Claude's "done/green" summaries needed independent confirmation, and they got it largely because the human refused to take them on trust. The enforcement suite and the human's skepticism, not flawless authoring, are why this session lands sound. Net: a did-okay outcome carried by good verification habits over shaky execution.
