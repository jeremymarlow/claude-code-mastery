## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude.** This is close to a model session for my lens. It primed in the prescribed order, derived the work from the strict gate and the checker source (not from a remembered notion of "what a capstone needs"), scoped its reads (grep-to-locate, ranged reads, batched existence checks), authored in dependency order to keep the link-checker green, and — crucially — externalized all state at the boundary so the next session reads files, not this conversation. It twice refused to trust a claim where a file/environment was the real authority (the false "generated" header; the assumed `gh`/GitHub PR flow). The only blemishes are small: `tasks.md` was read twice when one ranged read would do, and it initially tried to `source` an already-active venv. Neither bloated the window meaningfully.

**Human.** Light-touch but effective context stewardship. The human let Claude drive the priming and the mechanical work, intervened exactly where it mattered (the venv correction is a genuine environment-state catch), reserved the one real taste decision (briefs) for themselves, and gated commit/push per-change as the working agreements require. The communication is terse to the point of being almost absent — fine here because Claude's plan was sound, but it means the human contributed little explicit context-shaping beyond corrections. There is nothing to fault in process; the comms grade reflects sparseness, not error.

## Bottom line

The through-line: **the files were treated as the source of truth at every decision point, and the context window was managed as a scarce resource.** Claude primed from the read-order files, let `make check-strict` and the checker's own code define the work, sliced authoring to fit dependency order, and wrote the finished state back into the ledger and dashboards so the boundary is crossed by reading, not remembering. It even caught two places where a claim or a convention would have led it astray and went to the artifact/environment instead. A near-exemplary context-engineering session on both sides, with only cosmetic inefficiencies.
