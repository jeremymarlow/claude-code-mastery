1. **The transcript-tooling half is a near-textbook display of right-sized capability — and the architecture call is the standout.** When the user surfaced the "/export is lossy" problem, Claude resisted the temptation to over-build and instead split the work along the repo's own grain: a committed, pure-function Python tool (`tools/render-transcript`, `jsonl → md`) plus a *thin orchestrator* skill (`capture-session`), explicitly mirroring the existing `render-units`/`render-checklist` idiom. _Evidence:_ "Keep logic in a committed tool; make the skill a thin orchestrator — exactly like `render-units`/`render-index`/`render-checklist`" (turn at line 10564). This is the economical choice: the same tool serves both the live session and the back-fill, so one capability covers two jobs. [claude] [process]

2. **Session-identity resolution was investigated, not guessed — the single best economy decision of the session.** The genuinely hard requirement (robustly pick the right `.jsonl` when sessions run concurrently) could have shipped as a fragile mtime heuristic. Claude instead probed the environment and the event schema (`env | grep`, `jq 'keys'`) and found two robust signals — `$CLAUDE_CODE_SESSION_ID` and the per-event `sessionId` — then *verified the env var maps to the file* before building on it. _Evidence:_ "find ~/.claude/projects -name \"$CLAUDE_CODE_SESSION_ID.jsonl\" resolves the **exact** current transcript" (line 10699). This avoided a whole class of latent rework. [claude] [process]

## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** The tooling half is the high-water mark: investigate-before-building (session-id resolution, schema anatomy), right-sized architecture (committed tool + thin skill, matching repo idiom), defensive engineering (tolerant of unknown event types, no-clobber guard, clean STOP), and disciplined batching of tool calls with verification folded in. The security reasoning was genuinely careful — count-only scanning to avoid re-leaking into the live transcript, separating "by-convention" capture surfaces from "by-action" ones, and confirming the app's signing-secret handling rather than asserting it. The back-fill mapping refusal and the renderer-bug catch-and-fix both show the dogfooding loop paying off.

**Claude — weaknesses.** The U5–U16 editorial pass was executed serially when it was the textbook candidate for subagent delegation or per-turn batching — leverage left unused, and the cost-side mirror of running opus through bounded mechanical edits. The "thinking rendered 0 = bug" mislabel (quickly corrected) and the un-wired pre-commit hook (despite proposing it) are smaller process gaps. Communication is graded did-okay rather than did-well not for clarity — the summaries and fork-laying are excellent — but for *length*: many turns carry long multi-section wrap-ups (headers, tables, "decisions I need") that, in a 63%-context session, themselves spent budget; tighter closings would have been more economical.

**Human — strengths.** Sequencing and tooling judgment were consistently sharp: pausing for security at the exposure boundary, specifying the gate's human-in-the-loop form, choosing pause-on-everything with eyes open, directing feature-branch-then-fast-forward hygiene, and stress-testing the skill's durability (the no-arg naming question, the cross-machine clobber concern). The human treated the skill as a long-lived artifact, not a one-off — which is the right economic frame.

**Human — weaknesses.** The one-unit-at-a-time "continue to U#" cadence drove the serialized editorial pass; the human could have asked for a batched or delegated run of several units per turn, which would have spent fewer turns and less context for identical output. Minor, given the work was correct and the human stayed firmly in control of every gate.

## Bottom line

A two-mode session: a long, correct-but-serialized mechanical editorial pass, then a genuinely strong piece of tooling design. The through-line is *right-sized capability deployed deliberately* — investigated before built, scoped to the repo's idiom, and hardened against real failure modes (concurrency, cross-machine, secret leakage) — with the human supplying excellent sequencing and durability pressure. The economy is dented only by the editorial half: subagent/batching leverage went unused and opus ran the bounded mechanical work it didn't need to. Net, both parties used Claude Code close to its potential on the part that mattered most.
