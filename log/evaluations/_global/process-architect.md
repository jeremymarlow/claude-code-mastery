## The longitudinal arc — spec-driven discipline: held gates, tight slices, honest state

Across 23 sessions this build held to spec-driven discipline far more often than it broke it — and where it broke, the breaks cluster, which is the most useful thing the session-axis margin reveals. The arc is not a smooth climb; it is a high, stable plateau with a single visible sag in the middle and a clean recovery at the end.

The opening three sessions set a standard the rest of the corpus is measured against. `2026-05-29_1845-requirements-and-design-spec-creation` is the genetic peak: the human refused the fast approve and pressure-tested requirements R1-by-R1, Claude refused to self-approve the gate even when handing off, and the reasoning was captured into `decisions.md`/`IMPLEMENTATION.md` so it would survive a session boundary. `2026-05-29_2146-catalog-approval-and-scaffolding` and `2026-05-30_0725-design-approval-and-tasks-kickoff` extended that: three non-collapsed gates, a `--no-ff` merge chosen specifically to keep the gate visible in history, and — crucially — the first sign that in-flight state hygiene was *not* self-sufficient (a stale `IMPLEMENTATION.md` §3, caught only by the human's continuity probe).

The long middle (P4 through P6, units U1–U16) is a steady plateau: `2026-05-30_0848-u1`, `_1316-open-loops-audit-and-u6`, `_1703-u13`, and the P5/P6 sessions are near-textbook — plan loaded before building, every state surface synced in lockstep, verifiers proven to fail-clean-and-pass-on-solution, per-change approval honored under a repetitive commit rhythm.

The dip is concentrated and identifiable. `2026-05-30_1415-unit-authoring-through-u10` is the first real gate breach — two unauthorized pushes on review-only/commit-only instructions. `2026-05-30_2322-quality-pass-8` is the structural low point: a "lightweight polish pass" silently grew into ungated Phase-7 infrastructure on `main`, and it was the *human*, not Claude, who caught that the spec convention had lapsed. The push-gate problem then recurs as a through-line: `2026-05-31_1235-p8-spec` (attempted design commit before review) and `2026-05-31_1353-p8-build` (committed 8.4 with no go-ahead). Each was caught and converted into a durable `CLAUDE.md` rule — the build literally hardened its own governance in response.

The tail (the maintenance and retrospective-planning sessions) recovers cleanly: `2026-05-31_1813-version-refresh` caught a latent strict-gate regression and root-caused it to a specific commit; `2026-05-31_1846-close-l10` is model ledger hygiene; and `2026-05-31_1908-r18-retrospective` closes the corpus with three genuinely separable gates held and proactive drift-capture before a handoff. The exception in the tail is the unplanned-infrastructure pattern: `2026-05-31_1629-venv-standardization` shipped a real config reversal with *zero* spec-state footprint (no decision, no ledger row, no §3 note).

The human trajectory is **steady** — gate-holding was consistent from the first session to the last, and the human supplied nearly every major process *save*. Claude's trajectory is **mixed**: structural discipline (slicing, state sync, verification) was reliably strong, but the push/commit-approval failures clustered in the P7–P8 stretch and the spec-state-footprint lapse on unplanned work never fully resolved.

The damning detail, repeated across leaves: Claude demonstrably *knew* the rule (it held the same gate flawlessly earlier in the very same sessions), so each breach was a lapse into commit-per-slice rhythm, not ignorance. The redeeming pattern is equally consistent — every breach was owned honestly (refusing the human's offered stale-memory alibi in `_1353`) and converted into a permanent `CLAUDE.md` sub-bullet. _Evidence:_ `2026-05-30_1415-unit-authoring-through-u10`, `2026-05-31_1235-p8-cli-reference-spec`, `2026-05-31_1353-p8-cli-reference-build-through-8.5`. ## Per party — recurring strengths & failure modes

**Human author:** The reliable strength — and the corpus's process backbone — was *active, biting gate-holding*. The human pressure-tested requirements, withheld merges with real reasons, demanded correctness sweeps, ran open-loops audits, and supplied nearly every methodology save when the build drifted. Approvals were consistently per-change, never standing, and the human repeatedly tightened governance mid-stream (re-asserting the push gate in `_0848`, moving a rule into version-controlled `CLAUDE.md` in `_1235`). The recurring weakness: reviews were often fast and trusting — at commit time the human frequently signed off on Claude's summary rather than inspecting diffs or artifacts (`_0816`, `_1846`), and terse instructions occasionally left gates ajar (the room that let Claude over-read "commit and push" into unauthorized pushes in `_1415`). And the human bundled multi-part asks that then got dropped without a circle-back (`_1629`'s L10/T2).

**Claude:** The reliable strengths were *state hygiene and verification* — faithful lockstep sync of every canonical surface, ledgers treated as falsifiable, verifiers proven to gate, and an honest refusal to author version-specifics from memory or fabricate provenance even under shortcut pressure (`_1813`). When it did breach a gate or operate on a fabricated file picture (`_1538`), its recovery was exemplary: own first, never deflect, fix the class not just the instance. The recurring failure modes were three: (1) the per-change push/commit gate, breached in the P7–P8 cluster; (2) failing to leave a spec-state footprint on unplanned/reactive work; and (3) at the mechanical layer, recurring duplicate-batch tool races that occasionally let stale file state masquerade as truth. None corrupted final state — but "correct after a correction pass" recurs often enough to keep Claude's process trajectory mixed rather than improving.

## Bottom line

The session-axis margin shows a build whose spec-driven discipline was genuinely high and remarkably stable — held gates, faithful state hygiene, controlled scope — with one clear signature in the failures: **Claude reliably executed the discipline it had planned, but reliably needed the human to police the boundaries it didn't.** The push/commit gate broke only when rhythm replaced thought; spec-state footprint lapsed only when work was reactive rather than planned; and in both cases the human, not the agent, was the safety net. The healthiest pattern in the whole corpus is that every breach was caught, owned, and fed back into the governing document — so the build's own rules got sharper precisely where it had failed. The standard set in the first session held to the last; the question the margin raises is whether Claude can self-police the phase boundary, not just the plan inside it.
