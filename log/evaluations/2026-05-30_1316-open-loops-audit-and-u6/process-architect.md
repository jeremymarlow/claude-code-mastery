## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** This is a strong showing on every axis the lens cares about. The audit was substantive: ledger claims were independently falsified against CLI, git, and config state before being pronounced clean. Each unit slice was planned before it was built (TaskCreate plans matching what shipped), bounded to what one unit needs, verified end-to-end (fail-clean and pass-on-solution), and followed by a faithful sweep of every state surface — `IMPLEMENTATION.md` §3, the L1/L7 ledger rows, `tasks.md`, `SEEDED.md`, `version-record.md`, and memory all moved in lockstep, with no parallel-list drift. Approval was treated as per-change throughout. Lab-shape decisions were tied back to the design (R7.AC5/AC7/AC8) and recorded as decisions, not left implicit. Communication was crisp and decision-oriented: every unit ended with a "what shipped / verification / state synced / what's next" summary, and the L1 scope option and the unpushed-commit-before-clear were both surfaced proactively.

**Claude — weaknesses.** Genuinely minor. The recurring instinct to read files via `sed`/`grep` instead of the Read tool needed two nudges before it stuck — a habit, not a process failure, but it did cost two rejected tool calls. The literal-`{{vd:}}` trap was tripped twice (caught both times). And the session ran longer than the one-unit-per-slice protocol strictly implies; Claude's defense of continuing was reasonable but the human's tidiness instinct was arguably the more spec-faithful call.

**Human — strengths.** The human ran exactly the gates a Process Architect wants to see held. Method corrections were specific and well-timed (Read-over-sed, Read-before-Edit, the PIPESTATUS footgun) — real review, not rubber-stamping. Approval was issued per-change: separate, explicit go-aheads for each push, never a blanket "do whatever." The L1 scope decision was made decisively ("leave L1 as-is"). And the "fresh context?" question showed the human thinking about context hygiene as a first-class process concern.

**Human — weaknesses.** Very little to fault. The corrections, while correct, were reactive — a stricter upfront framing ("use Read tools, not shell, for file inspection") could have pre-empted the repeated nudges, but that is a coaching nicety, not a process gap. The human trusted Claude's per-unit state sweeps without spot-auditing the §3/ledger edits in-session, which was reasonable given Claude's demonstrated reliability but leaves the verification of state hygiene to a future session.
