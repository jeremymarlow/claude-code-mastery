## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** This is a strong spec-driven execution. Claude planned before building, grounded the plan in the strict gate's exact output rather than recollection, surfaced the single real decision for approval, sliced the work into per-artifact verified units, and kept every state file synchronized at the phase boundary with the ledger struck honestly. Its handling of the false "generated" header is the standout — it refused to ship a claim it knew to be untrue and fixed it at the root with a real, drift-gated tool, then disclosed the call plainly. Its commit/push discipline and the candid Gitea-vs-GitHub PR explanation show it treats outward-facing actions as gated, not assumed.

**Claude — weaknesses.** The final DoD sign-off ("all 11 Definition-of-Done items hold") leans on assertion for the content-only items rather than an explicit §6 walk. Given this is the v1 completion gate, that's the one place more ceremony was warranted. Also minor: two early bash calls hit friction (the `source venv` rejection; the literal `{{vd:key}}` tokens tripping the version-refs check twice) — small process noise, self-corrected, but a sharper read of the established conventions would have avoided them.

**Human — strengths.** The human ran a clean gate discipline: a deliberate "start the plan" instruction, a real approval on the brief-menu fork, and a scoped commit/push authorization that did not grant standing permission. The early correction ("you shouldn't need to source the venv, it's already active") kept Claude on the established workflow.

**Human — weaknesses.** Light-touch, which is mostly appropriate here, but the human approved the brief menu and then let the entire build (capstone + new tool + four state files) land before reviewing — a single large review surface at commit time rather than gates between the slices Claude had so carefully separated. The human also bundled "commit this and push" together, collapsing two gates the working agreements treat as distinct; Claude honored both, but the human's framing invited a looser reading. And the build-quality items of the capstone (rubric self-applicability, brief realism) were never independently reviewed by the human before push — they were trusted on Claude's say-so.

## Bottom line

A model spec-driven finalization session: plan derived from the source-of-truth strict gate, the one real decision gated for approval, work sliced and verified slice-by-slice, state files and the open-loops ledger synchronized honestly at the phase boundary, and a latent authenticity defect caught and fixed at the root with full disclosure. Both parties respected the per-change commit/push gate. The only real blemish is the v1 Definition-of-Done sign-off resting on assertion rather than an item-by-item §6 walk for the content obligations the static suite can't see — and a human review that trusted the build's quality rather than inspecting it before push.
