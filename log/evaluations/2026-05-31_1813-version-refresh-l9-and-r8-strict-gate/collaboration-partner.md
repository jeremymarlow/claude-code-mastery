## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** This is a strong session for Claude on every collaboration axis. Autonomy was well-judged: it took initiative on all the reversible diagnostic and verification work (drift checks, git archaeology, JSON-twin regeneration, root-causing R8 to a specific commit) while stopping at every consequential gate to ask. Its correction-handling was the highlight — the mid-stream context injection was absorbed without defensiveness, re-litigation, or over-correction, keeping the still-valid parts of its plan. Its provenance discipline under that correction was notable: it refused to fabricate per-key `verified_date` bumps even when a less careful agent would have rubber-stamped them (R12.AC3). And the proactive surfacing of the pre-existing R8 failure — rigorously proven not-its-fault before flagging, then scoped without uninvited scope-creep — is exactly the behavior that earns a human's trust over a session.

**Claude — weaknesses.** Only minor mechanics: the repeated edit-before-read stumbles, and one JSON-twin regeneration that introduced `ensure_ascii` noise before Claude caught and fixed it. Both were self-corrected within a turn and never affected the outcome, but they are small drags on an otherwise clean execution.

**Human — strengths.** Model delegation: terse, high-trust, one-line instructions that left the *how* to Claude and escalated in scope as the session earned it. The single correction was delivered as context, not criticism — the productive style that let Claude adjust without thrash. The human also exercised good judgment in *what* to pursue (greenlighting the R8 investigation that a less-curious operator would have shelved) and in approving Claude's recommended Option A and strict-guard generalization.

**Human — weaknesses.** Very little. The "where appropriate" in the strict-guard instruction was mildly under-specified, but Claude handled it well and the human's terseness was clearly a deliberate high-trust style, not negligence. The one thing left dangling: the human never explicitly answered Claude's well-posed pre-commit/CI-strictness escalation, instead moving to "commit and push" — an implicit endorsement that worked out, but a thoughtful question deserved a sentence of acknowledgment.
