## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — worked:** The core deliverable is correct and fit for purpose. The requirements are well-formed EARS, internally consistent, and survived a non-trivial mid-stream restructuring with no dangling references — and Claude *proved* this with real greps rather than claiming it. The session-boundary handoff package was a genuine piece of insight: it diagnosed the actual risk (lost rationale → re-litigated decisions) and built artifacts targeted at it. The "why it exists / what breaks without it / alternatives / my lean" template per requirement was a high-quality reasoning scaffold that made the human's job easy and the resulting decisions traceable.

**Claude — didn't:** Tool-batching hygiene was the weak spot — twice a fragile command in a parallel batch cancelled load-bearing writes, and only attentive recovery kept the final product whole. There was avoidable navigation churn (wasted re-reads, a double-run grep) late in the session. Neither corrupted the final artifacts, but both are the kind of execution sloppiness an outcome audit notes because they put the product at risk even when they don't damage it.

**Human — worked:** Drove outcome quality directly. The non-ranking-labels catch was a real save; the version-resilience reframing prevented a born-stale spec; the insistence on a self-sufficient handoff before the session boundary is exactly the instinct that makes a spec implementable rather than merely written. Communication was crisp and decision-complete — each reply resolved every open question Claude posed.

**Human — didn't:** The per-requirement review lost adversarial teeth as it went; from R3 onward the human mostly ratified Claude's pre-packaged recommendations verbatim. The decisions are defensible, but for an artifact sold on rigor, much of it rests on Claude's self-proposed leans rather than independent challenge.

## Bottom line

This session produced a real, sound artifact: a 15-requirement EARS spec that is internally consistent, verified with actual tooling, and wrapped in a handoff package deliberately engineered to survive an amnesiac session boundary. The work product holds up to independent inspection — claims match files, IDs are gap-free, cross-references survived a restructuring, and the status fields honestly reflect state. The human's R1 intervention and version pivot materially improved the result, though the review's two-party rigor thinned into near-verbatim assent over its back half. Net: a flawed path that nonetheless delivered a genuinely fit-for-purpose product — the case my lens credits.
