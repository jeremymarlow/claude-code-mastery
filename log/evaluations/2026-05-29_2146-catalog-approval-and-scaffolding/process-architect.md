## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude** ran this session the way the methodology is meant to run. It recognized the pre-design gate from project state rather than barreling into Phase 2; it held three sequential approval gates without collapsing them; it kept five state surfaces (`decisions.md`, `tasks.md`, `README.md`, `IMPLEMENTATION.md §3`, plus the design status markers) in continuous sync as work landed; and it logged every scope change — most notably the relaxation of the 12-unit cap — with rationale rather than absorbing it silently. The commit, when finally authorized, branched off `main` per convention and carried an accurate, scope-faithful message. The one genuine process weakness is within-phase slicing: eight design sections authored in a single uninterrupted run is a large slab to put in front of a reviewer, even though the human had explicitly asked Claude to push through to a session-complete state and Claude mitigated with a focused review guide.

**The human** deserves credit for the parts of process they own: they set a crisp session boundary (plan and design, defer commit to session end), they did not rubber-stamp the catalog gate (they reshaped it — security forward, unit cap dropped), and they chose to actually review the design rather than wave it through. They also correctly withheld the commit authorization until the work was presented, then gave it explicitly ("Please commit the changes"). The softer spot is communication: at the two discretionary forks the human delegated substantive curriculum-shape decisions back to Claude with minimal reasoning ("I leave it to your judgement," "all fine"). It worked because Claude surfaced the trade-offs and the human was reviewing concrete drafts — but it places most of the scope-discipline burden on Claude.

## Bottom line

This is a model spec-driven session: a real pre-design gate recognized from project state and honored, three non-collapsed approval gates that each produced a genuine human decision, continuous and honest state hygiene across every ledger and status marker, and scope changes captured with rationale rather than smuggled in. The commit was branched and deferred exactly as instructed. The only thing keeping this from flawless is the within-phase granularity — eight design sections in one pass is a big review slab — but that was a bounded, human-sanctioned trade-off, well mitigated by an explicit review guide. Both parties held the discipline the course exists to teach.
