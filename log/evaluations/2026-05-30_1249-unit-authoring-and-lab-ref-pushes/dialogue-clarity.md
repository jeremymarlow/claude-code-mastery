## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Human.** Instructions were short, scoped, and decisive throughout — "continue with the next unit," then a precise commit/push/memory-fix batch, then a one-line follow-up ("push start/u01-lab1 too to match"). The venv correction was a textbook clean correction: it named the problem, gave the exact remedy, and didn't assign blame. The one structural weakness is not the human's wording but the standing approval posture: the project's own CLAUDE.md says "Approval is per-change, not standing" and "Never push ... without explicit confirmation," and here the human gave a combined "commit and push" that Claude reasonably executed. That's fine — but the human also let Claude push a brand-new branch and tag under a single "push" without itemizing them, relying on Claude's judgment about *what* to push. It worked out because Claude checked remotes and announced its plan first, but the instruction left the scope of "push" implicit.

**Claude.** This is a strong communication session. Process discipline was high: it tested the reference implementation before structuring commits, proved the verifier *fails* on the clean state (not just passes on the solution), ran the full reset→fail→pass loop, and kept `make check` green at every gate. Reporting was lede-first and evidence-backed, failures were named honestly, and it surfaced a genuine tradeoff (the tag asymmetry) instead of silently deciding. Before pushing it inspected remotes and stated its intended push plan in prose — appropriate given the repo's push-caution rule. The blemish is the one over-confident, incorrect factual claim about the `start/u01-lab1` tag's remote status; the recovery (verify-then-correct) was exactly right, but the claim shouldn't have been asserted from an inference that didn't actually cover tags. Light narration is the only other minor drag.

## Bottom line

A clean, well-communicated build slice. The human gave scoped, decisive instructions and one exemplary mid-stream correction; Claude returned lede-first, evidence-grounded reports, surfaced a real decision point rather than complying silently, and reported its own missteps honestly. Both sides land in did-well territory, with communication a notch behind process on each side for the over-narration (Claude) and the implicit push-scope (human).
