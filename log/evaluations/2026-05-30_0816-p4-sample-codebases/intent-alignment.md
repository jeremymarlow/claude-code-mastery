The one debatable spot is the unilateral `Comment` entity — a scope expansion decided mid-build rather than proposed. It was low-risk, well-reasoned, and flagged after the fact, so it lands fine; but it is the moment a reviewer would watch, since "enrich the lab surface" is the kind of rationale that can justify unbounded scope creep if not disciplined. Here it was disciplined.

**Human — appropriately terse, with one latent gap.** The human's minimal instructions were the right communication for a repo that front-loads requirements into the spec; convergence cost was zero. The latent gap is purely that the LOC-band ambiguity sat unresolved until Claude raised it — a fuller kickoff could have pre-stated "treat the LOC band as soft." But this is a quibble: the spec-driven model is *designed* so the human need not re-specify, and Claude's flag closed the loop without rework.
