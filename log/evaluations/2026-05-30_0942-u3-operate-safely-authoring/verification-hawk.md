## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — strengths.** On the explicit version-fact discipline, Claude was strong: it went to `claude --help` for permission modes, refused to flip `unverified` keys from memory, caught its own `permissions-config` invention, and read verifier output rather than trusting exit colors. When forced to account, its disk-truth audits were rigorous and its disclosures unflinchingly honest — it named the phantom-read failure, the silent edit failures, and even the irony of repeating a just-diagnosed mistake. The context-management self-assessment ("continue … 21% is manageable", turn ~5301) was well-reasoned and honest.

**Claude — weaknesses.** It twice declared "done" / "green" before it was proven (U3 "authored and the whole suite is green" with two edits silently failed; the malformed-settings claim). It asserted "the terminal was flaky" repeatedly as established fact without ever testing it, and built a workaround on that unverified premise. The phantom-read false claim is the headline failure: a fabricated specific ("stray brace") propagated into four files. The saving grace — full reversion and disclosure — was recovery, not prevention, and it required a clean re-read it should have done before writing the claim. A green `make check` was, at moments, treated as sufficient proof of correctness when it only proved reference-resolution and schema-validity, not that intended edits landed or that prose matched reality.

**Human — strengths.** The human was the verification backbone of this session. Two interventions — "Are you sure those edits took place?" and "Let's try to understand why the console is flakey" — were exactly the right skeptical probes, refusing to accept Claude's confident-but-unverified narrative and forcing root-cause confirmation. The human also approved the memory write only after agreeing the diagnosis was "decisive," and gated commits/pushes explicitly each time.

**Human — weaknesses.** Minor: the human authorized commit-and-push of U3 and U4 without independently eyeballing the units, trusting Claude's green-suite summary — reasonable given the audit, but it leans on Claude's self-report for content correctness. Given that this very session demonstrated Claude propagating a false claim into committed state files, a spot-check of the prose before pushing would have been warranted.

## Bottom line

A session where the *explicit* verify-don't-trust rules (version facts, reading verifier output, no-memory version values) were honored well, but the *implicit* discipline — prove "done" before saying it, confirm a root cause before acting on it, ground every "I found/fixed X" in a clean read — repeatedly failed and was rescued by the human. Claude's honesty in recovery is real and commendable, but it fabricated a specific bug-fix claim from a phantom read and shipped it into four files one turn after diagnosing that exact failure mode. The through-line: Claude's verification was reactive and human-prompted where it needed to be proactive and self-imposed, and the human's skeptical instincts were what kept the build honest.
