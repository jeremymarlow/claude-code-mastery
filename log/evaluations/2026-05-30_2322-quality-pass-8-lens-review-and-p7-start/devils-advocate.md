## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**The human author — strong throughout, and the de-facto quality backstop.** The user's instincts repeatedly did the work the audit didn't. They pre-loaded the terseness and ID-confusion concerns into the depth question (turn "many of the unit files feel a bit terse… I don't typically recall the difference between… R1 vs U1 vs §1") — concerns that became the spine of the findings. They caught the `{{vd}}` rendering defect by actually reading the product in Gitea, which the 8-lens audit missed entirely. They forced the methodological correction ("we might have broken the convention of writing a spec") that turned a sprawling polish session back into a proper P7 phase. They governed scope sensibly (sandbox U1, gate before rollout, "show me the rendered U1 before I approve"). The one fair critique: "make every improvement you suggested unless you think that's too much" is a loose instruction that handed Claude the rope to build infrastructure under the banner of polish — a tighter brief might have forced the scope conversation earlier. But on balance the human drove the session's quality, not Claude.

**Claude — competent execution, weak self-criticism, scope discipline supplied by the user.** What worked: the functional run was clean and well-instrumented; it cleaned up its own stray `taskflow_app/` residue honestly (turn "it's my own residue"); the front-matter and "must it be at the top" investigations were properly grounded in code rather than guessed; once the user imposed P7 discipline, the spec-fold-in and clean-commit slicing were executed correctly and the working agreements (no commit/push without go-ahead, branch first) were respected. What didn't: the headline audit verdict was wrong on the one area that mattered; the lens that owned the defect declared it clean; the pedagogy judgment generalized from a thin read; the infrastructure scope creep was flagged only after shipping; and the U5–U16 commit shipped a structurally inconsistent state behind a green-but-blind gate. The throughline of Claude's weakness is the same as its rhetorical strength: it audits and narrates with great confidence, and that confidence outran its actual coverage more than once.
