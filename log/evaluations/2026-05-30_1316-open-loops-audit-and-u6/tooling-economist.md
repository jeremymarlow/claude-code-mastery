## Insights (ordered by significance)

1. **The defining tooling failure: reflexive `sed`/`awk`/`grep` for file reading, corrected by the human three separate times.** Claude opened the session reaching for `sed -n '1,120p'`, `awk '/Open loops/{f=1}...'`, and an `awk … sed` combo to *read* files — exactly when the Read tool was the right-sized instrument. The human had to reject the tool call twice ("explain why we are using shell and sed for reviewing files?" at the line-180 block; "please use the standard read tools when possible" at the P5 task-file block) and nudge a third time on a footgun. This is the cardinal sin of my lens: using a clumsier substitute for a job a dedicated tool does better — and worse, the project's own `CLAUDE.md` and the harness both push toward Read. The economic cost was real: rejected calls are wasted turns, and the second rejection cost an extra Read-MEMORY-Edit-MEMORY detour to record the feedback. _Evidence:_ "the tool use was rejected … and explain why we are using shell and sed for reviewing files?"; "please use the standard read tools when possible to read files. You often can't update them using the Update tools until they've been read anyway." [claude] [process]

The repetition was economical in *correctness* but not in *model spend*. _Evidence:_ the identical six-to-seven-file edit fan-out repeated for U6, U7, U8 (e.g. lines ~3870–3998). [claude] [process]

## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — worked:** The core build machinery was used to its potential. TodoWrite plans were right-sized and faithfully driven; the lab verifier red→green→reset loop was textbook and exactly the discipline the course teaches; git-ref choreography was flawless with no orphaned state; recovery from the vd-token failure was efficient (grep → Read the checker → one reworded edit). Communication was a genuine strength — crisp closings, honest counsel on context budget, and the rare good instinct to convert a repeated correction into a memory file.

**Claude — didn't:** The session's economic drag was tool-choice reflex. Reaching for `sed`/`awk`/`grep` to *read* files — against the project's own conventions and the harness's grain — cost three human interventions and a detour. Two ship-then-discover failures (PIPESTATUS, literal `{{vd:}}`) were avoidable with a beat of forethought. And there was leverage left unused: plan mode never gated the lab designs despite this being a plan-mode-teaching course; serialized Reads and one-at-a-time Edits where batching fit; and opus spent on mechanical state-doc propagation that a cheaper path or subagent could have absorbed.

**Human — worked:** A disciplined, economical reviewer. Per-change push approvals honored the project's no-standing-permission rule precisely; the tool-choice and PIPESTATUS nudges were the highest-leverage interventions available and delivered at the cheapest moment; the "fresh context?" question was a thoughtful prompt that surfaced good reasoning rather than a directive. Steered the audit-then-author sequence cleanly.

**Human — didn't:** Little to fault. The one observation: having corrected the read-tool reflex twice in one session, the human could have asked Claude to update the project `CLAUDE.md` (not just session-private memory) so the lesson persists across fresh sessions — the memory file Claude wrote lives in `~/.claude`, not the repo the next session reads first.

## Bottom line

A high-yield, well-communicated build session that shipped three verified units (U6 TDD, U7 debug, U8 git/PR) with flawless lab-ref choreography and exactly the red→green verification reflex the course preaches — dogfooding at its best. The recurring economic blemish was tool-choice reflex: `sed`/`grep` reached for where Read belonged, drawing three human corrections, plus two avoidable ship-then-discover failures. Capability *use* was strong where it counted (TodoWrite, verifiers, git refs) but left leverage unused at the edges — no plan-mode gate on lab designs, some serialized calls that could batch, and opus spent on rote propagation. The human's tight per-change approvals and pointed nudges were the quiet engine that kept the session economical.
