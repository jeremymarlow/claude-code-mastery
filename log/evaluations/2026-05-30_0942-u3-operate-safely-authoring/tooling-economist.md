## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

Communication under pressure was a strength — transparent about failures, well-reasoned on context management, and properly deferential on push permission.

**Claude — didn't:** Tool economy was poor for most of the session. Oversized duplicate batches produced a self-inflicted "flaky console" misdiagnosis and a wasteful `/tmp`-round-trip habit; dedicated `Read`/`Grep` were passed over for `cat`/`sed`/`awk`/`grep`-via-Bash against explicit guidance; subagents and plan mode — the two features that most directly fit this read-heavy, multi-AC authoring job — went unused. The phantom-read rework recurred even after being diagnosed.

**Human — worked:** Tight, well-sequenced direction ("check IMPLEMENTATION.md and what's next" → "start authoring U3" → reviewed → "commit… and push"). The two-step intervention on the "flaky console" claim was the session's best economic decision: refusing the symptom fix, naming a hypothesis, and insisting the diagnosis be saved to memory so future sessions don't pay the tax again. The author also correctly raised the context-budget question at a natural boundary.

**Human — didn't:** Little to fault. If anything, the inefficient Bash-over-dedicated-tools and duplicate-call pattern was visible from early turns and could have been flagged sooner, but the author caught it well within the session and converted it into durable process improvement.

## Bottom line

A high-quality *output* delivered through a *wasteful process*. The unit, lab, and state-sync are correct and the safety-critical verification was done right — but Claude spent much of the session fighting a console problem it had invented, reaching for Bash pipelines where dedicated tools fit, and never considering the subagent/plan-mode leverage that this exact kind of read-heavy, requirement-dense authoring is built for. The redeeming arc is the human's intervention and Claude's honest, auditable response: the root cause was correctly diagnosed and written to memory. That the same failure recurred one turn later is the clearest signal that the lesson needs to stick. Right model, right verification depth; wrong tools and wasteful turns everywhere in between.
