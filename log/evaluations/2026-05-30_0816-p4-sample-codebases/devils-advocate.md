## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Claude — worked.** Orientation was disciplined: it read `IMPLEMENTATION.md`, the P4 task file, design §7, the existing `verify-lab`/`reset-lab`, and the L4 ledger row *before* writing a line — so the build was grounded in the files, not memory, exactly as CLAUDE.md demands. The behavioral verification of the three seeded bugs (insight 2) and the in-memory-DB smoke test (insight 1) are real, independent checks that caught a genuine wiring bug. State bookkeeping was meticulous and honest: boxes checked, L4 struck through, L7 opened for the deferred lab tags, decisions recorded with rationale, and the two judgment calls surfaced plainly to the human rather than buried. The closing summary is a model of disclosure.

**Claude — didn't.** The unit suite is self-confirming and was oversold as proof of correctness (insight 1). Two prose-quality defects (the foreign-language leak, the line-number drift) reached deliverables and were caught only by Claude's own re-reads, not by any gate — evidence that prose was emitted faster than verified (insights 3, 4). The LOC reinterpretation and the compensating scope creep are a minor internal contradiction (insights 5, 6). And the answer-key's line-number anchoring is a latent rot source nobody questioned.

**Human — worked.** Held the commit gate correctly (waited for explicit "commit"), and the minimal prompting gave Claude room to execute a large slice cleanly.

**Human — didn't.** Provided no review of any artifact and did not engage with either explicitly-flagged judgment call before authorizing the commit. The session's quality rests almost entirely on the model's self-discipline; the human added no independent check on bug realism, test meaningfulness, spec-conformance, or prose accuracy.

## Bottom line

A genuinely strong build session whose strength is narrower than the summary suggests. The defensible core — orientation from the files, behavioral reproduction of the seeded bugs, a smoke test that caught a real bug, and scrupulous state/ledger hygiene — is real skill and earns a high mark for Claude. But the green test suite proves correctness it didn't actually demonstrate, two prose defects reached deliverables and survived only because Claude proof-read itself, and a spec number was reinterpreted downward by a third with the human ratifying everything unseen. The good outcome leans heavily on the model's conscientiousness rather than on any human oversight or external gate; swap in a less careful model and the same human inputs ship a worse artifact. Strong, but earned by Claude's diligence, not by the process having any redundancy.
