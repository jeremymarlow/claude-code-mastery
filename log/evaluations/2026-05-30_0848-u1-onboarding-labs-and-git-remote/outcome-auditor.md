**Claude — strengths.** This is a strong outcome session. Every artifact that was claimed "done" was actually exercised: the verifier was run in both states, the lab git refs were round-tripped through `reset-lab`, `make check` was confirmed green at each commit (and the pre-commit hook independently re-ran it), and the traceability PENDs were read correctly (C1/C2 dropping off the "not practiced by a lab" list is real confirmation, not a guess). The information-gathering before authoring was disciplined — Claude read the schema, the templates, the relevant checks (`check-frontmatter`, `check-links`, `check-coverage`, `check-traceability`), and the actual codebase files before grounding the answer key, which is why the U2 answer key is correct rather than plausible. The two judgment calls that most expose an outcome auditor — refusing to fake-verify `search-refs`, and not fabricating a `log/` session transcript it couldn't authentically produce — both came down on the side of honesty over a clean-looking "done."

**Claude — weaknesses.** Very little to fault on the product. The closest thing to a gap is that the session's correctness rests partly on `pytest` actually running in the verifier's environment — confirmed here ("deps OK"), but the `verify.sh` assumes deps are installed and only soft-points at doctor; a learner with a broken venv would get a pytest error rather than a clean "set up your environment first" message. That's a robustness nit on an otherwise sound artifact, not a defect.

**Human — strengths.** The human steered with clean, decisive instructions ("start U1," "commit U1 and create the lab refs," "start U2," then a precise push instruction) and, importantly, gated commits/pushes per-change rather than handing over standing permission — Claude paused after authoring U1 to ask before committing, and the human's explicit "always ask before pushing" reinforced exactly the right boundary. The closing instruction ("review our work, ensure status is captured, capture knowledge for future sessions") triggered the most valuable correctness work of the session (the stale-memory fix).

**Human — weaknesses.** None material. The human trusted Claude to interpret "our changes" for the push, which worked out, but a slightly more explicit scope ("the U2 commit and the U1 lab refs") would have removed even the small inference Claude had to make.
