## What worked / what didn't (both parties)

**Human — strengths.** The human ran a disciplined, gate-respecting session: every state-mutating action was individually approved, and the mid-stream context injection ("I stopped you to give you extra context") was exactly the right intervention at the right moment — it prevented Claude from spending effort re-verifying keys that didn't need it, and clarified the *intent* behind the 2.1.158 hold that no file fully captured. The instinct to generalize the R8 fix into a standing guard ("update the skill or CLAUDE.md… to use strict") shows the human thinking in terms of recurrence-prevention, not just the immediate red check. Direction was terse but unambiguous.

**Human — weaknesses.** Little to fault for this session's scope. The deepest process observation is upstream of this session: the R8 regression existed because P7's "done" was gated on plain `make check`, and the human approved that — but this session is precisely where the human caught and corrected that latent gap, so it reads as credit, not debt. The human accepted the "push landed" confirmations on the `git status -sb` evidence without asking to see the truncated push output; minor, and the subsequent `git log` confirmed the commits.

**Claude — strengths.** Forensic rigor was the throughline: it never asserted state from memory, it proved merge-containment, it stashed-and-retested to attribute the R8 failure correctly, it bisected to the exact commit, and it guarded diff cleanliness on the JSON twin. The provenance discipline under a shortcut-tempting refresh (not fabricating per-key verification dates) is the single best demonstration of the spec's honesty rules being internalized rather than performed. State files were updated as work landed (§3, ledger L6/L9, decisions entries), not left to drift — and it actively hunted for parallel-list drift (grep for stale L6 references after striking it).

**Claude — weaknesses.** Genuinely minor. Several confirmations leaned on `git status -sb` as proxy for push success because the real output was truncated to a persisted file Claude chose not to open. Early in the session a couple of Edit calls failed the read-before-write precondition (version-data.yaml, version-record.md) and had to Read-then-retry — harmless friction, but it shows the edit/read state-tracking occasionally lagged. Neither touched correctness or state integrity.
